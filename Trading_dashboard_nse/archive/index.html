📚 Trading Strategy Guide

🎯 How Our Strategy Engine Works

Our intelligent trading strategy system analyzes real-time market data across multiple dimensions to provide actionable trading recommendations. The system evaluates volatility, market trends, breadth, and power dynamics to suggest optimal strategies.

📈 Trend-Based Strategies

  • Strong Bullish (>60% breadth): Momentum trading, buying dips, call options on strong stocks
  • Bullish: Position trading with upside bias, selective buying
  • Bearish: Defensive positioning, reduced exposure
  • Strong Bearish (<40% breadth): Short selling, put options, inverse ETFs
  • Neutral: Wait and watch, sectoral rotation

📊 Volatility-Based Strategies

  • High Volatility (>2%): Straddle/Strangle options, scalping, tight stop losses
  • Moderate Volatility (1-2%): Swing trading, normal position sizing
  • Low Volatility (<1%): Range trading, iron condor, theta decay strategies, premium selling

🎭 Market Breadth Indicators

  • >65% Advancers: Overbought conditions, watch for profit booking
  • 55-65% Advancers: Healthy bullish trend
  • 45-55% Advancers: Neutral/Consolidation phase
  • 35-45% Advancers: Bearish trend developing
  • <35% Advancers: Oversold, potential bounce

⚡ Bull/Bear Power Analysis

  • Net Power >20: Strong institutional buying, follow the leaders
  • Net Power -10 to +10: Balanced market, stock-specific action
  • Net Power <-20: Distribution phase, defensive positioning
  • Extreme Power (>30): Watch for exhaustion and reversal

⏱️ Timeframes

  • Scalping: Minutes to hours, quick profits
  • Intraday: Same day entry/exit
  • Short-term: 1-5 days holding period
  • Medium-term: 1-4 weeks
  • Long-term: Months to years

⚠️ Risk Management

  • Position Sizing: Reduce size in high volatility
  • Stop Losses: Always use, tighten in volatile markets
  • Diversification: Don't put all capital in one trade
  • Risk/Reward: Aim for minimum 1:2 ratio
  • Max Risk: Never risk >2% of capital per trade

🎓 Strategy Definitions

Momentum Trading: Buying stocks showing strong upward price movement with high volume.

Range Trading: Buying at support and selling at resistance in sideways markets.

Options Strategies: Using calls, puts, spreads, and combinations for directional or neutral plays.

Scalping: Very short-term trades capturing small price movements.

Iron Condor: Selling out-of-money put and call spreads in low volatility.

Straddle/Strangle: Buying both call and put options to profit from large moves in either direction.
