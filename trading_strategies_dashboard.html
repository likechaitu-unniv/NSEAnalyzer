Bullish Strategies
Use when market sentiment is positive & you expect price to rise
📈
Bull Call Spread
Bullish

Buy a lower-strike call and sell a higher-strike call with the same expiry. Reduces premium cost while capping upside profit. Ideal when you expect moderate upside movement.

BUY Call (lower strike) SELL Call (higher strike)
Max ProfitStrike Diff − Net Debit
Max LossNet Premium Paid
BreakevenLower Strike + Net Debit
Best ForModerate bullish move
ComplexityLow

Long Call
Bullish

Simply buy a call option to gain unlimited upside exposure. Premium is the only cost. Works best when you anticipate a sharp, swift rally — time decay works against you.

BUY Call (ATM or OTM)
Max ProfitUnlimited
Max LossPremium Paid
BreakevenStrike + Premium
Best ForStrong directional move
ComplexityVery Low

Covered Call
Bullish

Hold underlying (or futures) and sell an OTM call to generate income. Caps upside but reduces effective cost basis. Most popular income strategy among retail traders.

HOLD Underlying / Futures SELL Call (OTM)
Max ProfitStrike − Cost + Premium
Max LossCost − Premium Received
BreakevenCost − Premium
Best ForSideways-to-mild bullish
ComplexityMedium

Bull Put Spread (Credit Spread)
Bullish

Sell a higher-strike put and buy a lower-strike put. Collect net premium upfront. Profits when the underlying stays above the short put strike at expiry.

SELL Put (higher strike) BUY Put (lower strike)
Max ProfitNet Credit Received
Max LossStrike Diff − Net Credit
BreakevenShort Put − Net Credit
Best ForMild bullish / low IV
ComplexityMedium

Call Ratio Back Spread
Bullish

Sell 1 lower-strike call, buy 2 (or more) higher-strike calls. Benefits from a very large upward move. Can be set up for a net credit. Carries risk in the middle zone at expiry.

SELL 1× Call (lower strike) BUY 2× Call (higher strike)
Max ProfitUnlimited (above breakeven)
Max LossAt higher strike at expiry
BreakevenHigher Strike + Max Loss
Best ForBig breakout expected
ComplexityHigh

Synthetic Long
Bullish

Buy ATM call + sell ATM put at same strike. Mimics long futures exposure using options. Smaller upfront margin. Profits and losses are similar to owning the underlying.

BUY ATM Call SELL ATM Put
Max ProfitUnlimited
Max LossLarge (below breakeven)

Best ForStrong bullish conviction

Neutral / Range-Bound Strategies
Use when the market is expected to stay within a range or consolidate
⚖️
Short Straddle
Neutral

Sell an ATM call and ATM put at the same strike. Profit from time decay when the market stays sideways. High risk if market makes a big move in either direction.

SELL ATM Call SELL ATM Put
Max ProfitTotal Premium Collected
Max LossUnlimited (both sides)
BreakevenStrike ± Total Premium
Best ForLow VIX / consolidation
ComplexityMedium
🤝
Short Strangle
Neutral

Sell OTM call and OTM put at different strikes. Wider profit zone than short straddle but lower premium collected. Popular with weekly expiry traders on Nifty.

SELL OTM Call SELL OTM Put
Max ProfitTotal Premium Collected
Max LossUnlimited (both sides)
BreakevenOTM Strike ± Total Premium
Best ForRange-bound, low VIX
ComplexityMedium
🦅
Iron Condor
Neutral

Combine a Bull Put Spread + Bear Call Spread. Defined risk, defined reward. Wide profit zone. Extremely popular on weekly Nifty expiry for consistent income generation.

SELL OTM Put BUY Lower Put SELL OTM Call BUY Higher Call
Max ProfitNet Credit Received
Max LossStrike Diff − Net Credit
BreakevenBetween 2 breakevens
Best ForSideways expiry play
ComplexityMedium
🦋
Iron Butterfly
Neutral

Sell ATM straddle + buy OTM wings for protection. Higher premium collected than Iron Condor but narrower profit zone. Profits maximally when price expires exactly at ATM strike.

BUY OTM Put SELL ATM Put SELL ATM Call BUY OTM Call
Max ProfitNet Credit (at ATM expiry)
Max LossWing Width − Net Credit
BreakevenATM Strike ± Net Credit
Best ForPinning to a level
ComplexityHigh
📅
Calendar Spread (Time Spread)
Neutral

Sell a near-term option and buy a same-strike far-term option. Profits from time decay difference between two expiries. Low capital requirement, theta decay is your friend.

SELL Near-Expiry Option BUY Far-Expiry Option
Max ProfitLimited (near expiry time)
Max LossNet Debit Paid
BreakevenDepends on IV difference
Best ForLow IV, sideways market
ComplexityHigh

Long Call Butterfly
Volatile

Buy lower call, sell 2 middle calls, buy higher call. Low cost, defined risk. Profits near the middle strike at expiry. Use when IV is high and you expect a calm expiry later.

BUY 1× Call (low) SELL 2× Call (mid) BUY 1× Call (high)

Max LossNet Debit Paid
BreakevenLower + Net Debit & Upper − Net Debit
Best ForPost-event IV crush
ComplexityHigh

Diagonal Spread
Volatile

Buy a longer-dated option and sell a nearer-dated option at a different strike. Combines benefits of calendar and vertical spreads. Versatile — can adapt to changing outlook.

BUY Far-Expiry Option (lower strike) SELL Near-Expiry Option (higher strike)
Max ProfitVaries by expiry & IV
Max LossNet Debit Paid
BreakevenRequires modelling
Best ForIV skew exploitation
ComplexityVery High

Risk Management Strategies
Protect your capital and limit losses regardless of market direction
🚦
Stop-Loss Orders
Risk Mgmt

Automatically exit a losing position when it hits a pre-defined price. The most fundamental risk management tool. Use hard SL for options (e.g. 50% of premium paid) and trailing SL for futures.

TypeHard / Trailing SL
Options SL RuleExit at 50% of premium paid
Futures SL Rule20–30 points from entry
Risk ReductionHigh
ComplexityVery Low

Position Sizing (1–2% Rule)
Risk Mgmt

Never risk more than 1–2% of total capital on a single trade. Prevents a single bad trade from causing irreparable damage to your portfolio. Apply consistently across all strategies.

RuleRisk ≤ 1–2% per trade

Pro Tips & Best Practices
Key principles every NSE options trader should follow
📅 Expiry Timing
  • Sell premium 7–10 DTE for maximum theta decay
  • Avoid holding short options into last 1–2 days (gamma risk)
  • Use weekly expiry for income; monthly for swing trades
  • Roll positions 2–3 DTE if still profitable
📊 IV-Based Selection
  • India VIX > 20: Sell premium strategies (Condor, Strangle)
  • India VIX < 15: Buy options (Straddle, Strangle, Spreads)
  • Avoid buying straddles when IV is already elevated
  • Check IV Percentile before every trade entry
🎯 Strike Selection
  • Short Strangle: sell 1 standard deviation OTM strikes
  • Iron Condor: use 10–15 delta for short strikes
  • Credit spreads: collect at least 1/3 of the width
  • Respect max pain and strong support/resistance levels
🛡️ Trade Management
  • Take profits at 50% of max credit on winning positions
  • Close losing positions at 200% of credit received (2× loss)
  • Adjust positions when price breaches short strike
  • Avoid over-adjusting — sometimes letting go is better
🏦 Capital Allocation
  • Never deploy more than 50% of capital in one expiry
  • Keep 20–30% in cash as a buffer for adjustments
  • Allocate separately for intraday vs swing vs positional
  • Track overall portfolio delta, not just per-trade
📰 Event Calendar
  • RBI Policy: Buy straddle 3–4 days before; exit on event day
  • Budget: Volatility spikes — avoid short premium entry before
  • FII Data (Wednesday): Watch for large OI shifts
  • US Fed meetings can affect Nifty opening significantly

Legend & Key Terms
Quick reference for common options terminology
ATM — At the Money (strike = spot price)
ITM — In the Money (call: strike < spot)
OTM — Out of the Money (call: strike > spot)
IV — Implied Volatility of an option
PCR — Put-Call Ratio (open interest based)
DTE — Days to Expiry
Theta — Daily time decay of an option
Delta — Sensitivity to price movement
Gamma — Rate of change of delta
Vega — Sensitivity to IV change
Max Pain — Strike where options buyers lose max
OI — Open Interest in options chain
