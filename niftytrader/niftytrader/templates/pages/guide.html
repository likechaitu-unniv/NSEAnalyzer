Getting Started with NiftyTrader

Welcome to NiftyTrader! This guide will help you get started with our market analysis dashboard.

What is NiftyTrader?

NiftyTrader is an advanced dashboard for analyzing Nifty 50 stocks and market trends with real-time data.

Key Sections

  • Dashboard: Real-time stock data for all Nifty 50 companies
  • Trends: Technical analysis and market sentiment
  • Guide: Educational resources and tools

Dashboard Guide

Learn how to navigate and interpret the stock dashboard.

Market Overview Cards

At the top of the dashboard, you'll see four key metrics:

  • Market Trend: Overall market direction (Bullish/Bearish)
  • Average Change: Average percentage change across stocks
  • Advancers/Decliners: Number of up vs down stocks
  • Bull/Bear Power: Weighted strength of bulls vs bears

Stock Table

The main table shows all Nifty 50 stocks with:

  • Symbol: Stock ticker
  • Price: Current market price
  • Change: Absolute change in points
  • % Change: Percentage change
  • Weight: Stock's weight in the index
  • Volume: Trading volume

Trends Analysis Guide

Understanding market trends is crucial for making informed trading decisions.

Analysis Metrics

  • Market Signal: Composite signal (Bullish/Bearish/Neutral)
  • PCR: Put-Call Ratio indicator of sentiment
  • Support/Resistance: Key price levels
  • Open Interest: Market depth indicator

How to Use

  1. Select an expiry date
  2. Review the current signal
  3. Check support and resistance levels
  4. Compare with historical trends

Trading Glossary

Key Terms

PCR (Put-Call Ratio)

The ratio of put options to call options. High PCR indicates bearish sentiment, low PCR indicates bullish sentiment.

OI (Open Interest)

The total number of outstanding derivative contracts. Indicates market participation and liquidity.

IV (Implied Volatility)

Market expectation of volatility. Higher IV means market expects larger price swings.

Support Level

Price level where buying interest emerges, preventing further price decline.

Resistance Level

Price level where selling interest emerges, preventing further price rise.

Market Breadth

Number of advancing stocks vs declining stocks. Confirms trend strength.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Nifty 50?

Nifty 50 is a benchmark index of 50 large-cap Indian companies listed on NSE. It represents the Indian stock market.

How often is data updated?

Data updates in real-time during market hours (9:15 AM - 3:30 PM IST on weekdays). Outside market hours, data represents the last available market close.

What do the colors represent?

Green indicates positive change/bullish movement. Red indicates negative change/bearish movement. Grey indicates neutral or no change.

Is this investment advice?

No. NiftyTrader is for educational and informational purposes only. Always consult with a licensed financial advisor before making investment decisions.

Can I trade based on these signals?

The signals are analytical tools. Do your own research and risk management before trading. These signals are not guaranteed predictions of future price movements.
